# Type Pack: Beloved Duke / Frontier Healing

Use for 溺愛, 辺境伯, frontier count, cursed prince or duke, exile to remote estate, new home, healing romance, and protective male lead.

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as structural references: exile/home-building, misunderstood duke, estate problem, protection scene, and healing ladder. If available, read `对标/beloved-duke-frontier/{work}/拆文报告.md`.

## Core Engine

- The heroine arrives wounded, exiled, disgraced, poor, or unwanted.
- The frontier or duke household must have a concrete problem: curse, debt, monster border, neglected estate, bad staff, trade route, winter, succession, or rumors.
- The male lead is powerful but emotionally blocked, cursed, feared, or socially isolated.
- Romance develops through safety plus usefulness: she gains a home by solving real problems, not by being decorative.
- The payoff is "I was unwanted there, but I am necessary and loved here."

## Six-Episode Season

1. Arrival after rejection: heroine enters the frontier/duke house under a wound or rumor.
2. First useful act: she solves a small household, territory, curse, or reputation problem.
3. External contempt: the old family, capital, fiance, or noble faction belittles the new place.
4. Protective recognition: the male lead publicly values her specific contribution.
5. Estate/family test: the frontier faces a larger problem only her agency can unlock.
6. Home declared: the old rejection loses power and the heroine chooses the new place and love.

## Required Payoffs

- Old wound.
- New-home problem.
- Heroine competence.
- Male-lead protection plus respect.
- Estate/territory consequence.
- Emotional homecoming.

## Avoid

- Frontier as only scenery.
- Male lead loving her for being pitiful.
- Healing the duke without the heroine having a future of her own.
- Territory, home-building, food, or community success replacing the adult couple's decisive relationship choice.
