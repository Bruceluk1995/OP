# Type Pack: Craft / Job Competence / Shop Romance

Use for 職人, 杖職人, potion shop, magic tool repair, cooking, accounting, 魔力ゼロ, unwanted job, shopkeeping, and competence-based romance.

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as structural references: craft rule, customer problem, false evaluation, product proof, reputation economy, and slow romance through work. If available, read `对标/craft-job-competence/{work}/拆文报告.md`.

## Core Engine

- The heroine is underestimated because her job, attribute, magic level, or background looks unimpressive.
- Each episode needs a concrete product or service: wand, potion, dress, ledger, food, herb, charm, repair, translation, etiquette lesson, or magic tool.
- The product must solve a visible problem and alter reputation, money, access, romance, or risk.
- Romance comes from a customer, noble, duke, knight, or rival craftsperson who respects the work before worshipping the person.
- The zamaa is often reputational or economic: the old evaluators lose clients, proof, status, or credibility.

## Six-Episode Season

1. False evaluation: heroine is dismissed as useless or low value, then opens/uses a craft path.
2. First customer proof: a product solves a small but concrete problem.
3. Reputation pressure: a rival, family, guild, or noble tries to dismiss or steal the work.
4. High-stakes commission: the male lead or institution needs her exact skill.
5. Public product proof: test, auction, ceremony, illness, battle support, or court order validates the craft.
6. New standing: heroine gains shop, title, guild status, patron, or chosen partner.

## Required Payoffs

- Named craft/job.
- Product or service with rule.
- Customer/witness reaction.
- Money, reputation, access, or legal consequence.
- Romance through respect for work.

## Avoid

- Craft scenes that only list cozy actions.
- "She is talented" without a product test.
- Treating job competence as side decoration after romance.
