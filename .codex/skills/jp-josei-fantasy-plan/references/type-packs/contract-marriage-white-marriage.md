# Type Pack: Contract Marriage / White Marriage

Use for 契約結婚, 白い結婚, political marriage, temporary wife, cursed duke husband, arranged marriage, and daily care turning into devotion.

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as structural references: contract clause, forced proximity, household pressure, public-wife role, daily-care ladder, and eventual open choosing. If available, read `对标/contract-marriage-white-marriage/{work}/拆文报告.md`.

## Core Engine

- The relationship starts with explicit terms: duration, no love, heir issue, debt, reputation repair, curse cure, estate management, or political cover.
- Every episode should pressure one clause of the contract.
- The heroine's value appears through household work, negotiation, healing, accounts, etiquette, territory management, or emotional steadiness.
- The male lead's love grows through specific recognition: he changes a rule, protects her status, remembers a detail, or lets her into a guarded space.
- The payoff is the shift from "contract obligation" to "chosen partner".

## Six-Episode Season

1. Contract signed: terms are harsh, practical, or loveless, but the heroine chooses it for a reason.
2. Household proof: she solves one concrete estate, staff, curse, debt, or reputation problem.
3. Clause pressure: the contract is used against her or creates an emotional boundary.
4. Private recognition: the male lead changes behavior because he has seen her specific strength.
5. Public wife test: salon, family, court, or church challenges the marriage's legitimacy.
6. Chosen marriage: contract terms are rewritten, discarded, or publicly exceeded.

## Required Payoffs

- Named contract clause.
- Shared space.
- Practical heroine competence.
- Male-lead rule change.
- Public wife/husband recognition.
- Choice beyond obligation.

## Avoid

- Instant溺愛 with no forced-proximity ladder.
- Contract terms that never matter.
- Making the heroine a household saint with no personal desire.
- Turning contract marriage into a step-parenting, childcare, custody, adoption, guardian, or family-reconstruction story whose adult romance is optional.
