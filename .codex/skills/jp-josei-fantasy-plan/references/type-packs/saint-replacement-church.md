# Type Pack: Saintess Replacement / Church Proof

Use for 聖女, 偽聖女, saintess replacement, church politics, healing proof, holy magic, purification, and knight/duke protection.

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as structural references: false evaluation, church inquiry, ritual proof, healing consequence, and public restoration. If available, read `对标/saint-replacement-church/{work}/拆文报告.md`.

## Core Engine

- The heroine is called fake, useless, low-magic, cursed, or replaceable.
- Her real value must be specific: healing method, purification rule, herb knowledge, prayer condition, record keeping, curse reading, or miracle with a cost.
- The church or court creates pressure through rank, doctrine, money, witnesses, and official records.
- Romance comes from a knight, duke, prince, or mage who protects the proof process, not only the heroine's body.
- Zamaa lands when the institution has to admit its own false evaluation or corruption.

## Six-Episode Season

1. Replacement: the heroine is dismissed or accused as fake while a new saintess is celebrated.
2. First proof: a small healing, purification, or record contradiction shows the truth to one witness.
3. Institutional pressure: church officials, nobles, or the replacement saintess try to control the proof.
4. Cost of ability: the heroine's power has a personal or social cost, and the male lead responds with respect.
5. Public ritual or inquiry: a ceremony, trial, plague, curse outbreak, or holy relic exposes the lie.
6. Restoration and chosen future: reputation is repaired, the corrupt side faces consequence, and the romance becomes public.

## Required Payoffs

- False evaluation.
- Concrete healing or purification rule.
- Church/court record.
- Witnessed proof.
- Male-lead protection of process.
- Institutional consequence.

## Avoid

- "She is the real saintess" without showing how the power works.
- Healing as a vague glow with no cost, condition, or consequence.
- Church villains who are evil without any social mechanism.
- A church-proof story whose outcome and climax remain intact after removing the primary love interest's costly recognition or partnership.
