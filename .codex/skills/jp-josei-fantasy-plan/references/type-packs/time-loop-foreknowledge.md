# Type Pack: Time Loop / Foreknowledge

Use for ループ, やり直し, regression, death flags, tragedy prevention, second chance, execution avoidance, and future knowledge.

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as structural references: death trigger, known flag, changed choice, unexpected ripple, and romance through a changed timeline. If available, read `对标/time-loop-foreknowledge/{work}/拆文报告.md`.

## Core Engine

- The heroine knows a future wound: death, execution, exile, broken engagement, family ruin, curse outbreak, war, or betrayal.
- Each episode should target one flag and show one changed choice.
- Foreknowledge must create new uncertainty; the story dies if every future event is solved too easily.
- Romance works when the male lead notices behavior that differs from the old timeline, or when the heroine must decide whether to trust him with the truth.
- Zamaa is often delayed: preventing tragedy first, exposing the cause later.

## Six-Episode Season

1. Return point: heroine wakes before ruin and changes one immediate flag.
2. First ripple: the changed choice creates a new problem or ally.
3. Trust pressure: she needs help but cannot fully explain the loop.
4. Flag collision: two future events conflict and force a costly choice.
5. Hidden cause: evidence reveals who benefits from the original tragedy.
6. Future broken: the expected ruin is avoided, but a larger timeline mystery remains.

## Required Payoffs

- Return/death flag.
- Changed action.
- Unexpected ripple.
- Trust decision.
- Evidence of hidden cause.
- A future that is no longer guaranteed.

## Avoid

- Solving everything by memory.
- Repeating the old-life summary instead of staging current choices.
- Romance that ignores the heroine's fear of the timeline.
- A tragedy-prevention plot whose decisive changed future does not depend on adult romantic trust or re-choice.
