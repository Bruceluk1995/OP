# Type Pack: Engagement Annulment / Zamaa

Use for 婚約破棄, public accusation, stolen fiance, false saintess or sister rivalry attached to a broken engagement, and clear social reversal.

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as structural references: public humiliation venue, evidence chain, witness design, better-match promise, and social/legal consequence. If available, read `对标/engagement-annulment-zamaa/{work}/拆文报告.md`.

## Core Engine

- The heroine is humiliated in a public venue where reputation matters.
- The accusation contains a flaw, lie, stolen credit, or illegal maneuver that can later be proved.
- The heroine keeps agency: she does not only cry; she preserves evidence, chooses silence strategically, names a witness, or accepts a new condition.
- The romance payoff comes from a man who sees the truth before society does.
- The zamaa payoff must follow evidence, witness, law, contract, church record, account book, magic proof, or court etiquette.

## Six-Episode Season

1. Public annulment: accusation lands, heroine notices the lie, and a better protector or witness appears.
2. Evidence seed: the heroine retrieves a record, contract clause, letter, or magic trace while rumors worsen.
3. Better match pressure: the male lead offers fair shelter or partnership, creating a new political problem.
4. Antagonist overreach: the fiance, sister, or faction tries to erase evidence and exposes another lie.
5. Public inquiry: witnesses, records, and etiquette turn against the antagonist.
6. Reversal and choice: broken engagement becomes the heroine's freedom, the new romance is publicly chosen, and a next-status hook remains.

## Required Payoffs

- Public wound.
- Evidence object.
- Witness or legal venue.
- Heroine choice with cost.
- Better-match recognition.
- Social consequence that fits the setting.

## Avoid

- Zamaa based only on author narration.
- A heroine who contributes no proof or choice.
- A replacement fiance who is punished only because the male lead is stronger.
