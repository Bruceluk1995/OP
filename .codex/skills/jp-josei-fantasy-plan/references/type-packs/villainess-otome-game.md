# Type Pack: Villainess / Otome Game / Manga Reincarnation

Use for 悪役令嬢, 乙女ゲーム, manga or novel reincarnation, route knowledge, death flags, fate avoidance, and heroine who changes a scripted role.

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as structural references: original-route pressure, flag recognition, social role reversal, non-main-route romance, and fate-avoidance rhythm. If available, read `对标/villainess-otome-game/{work}/拆文报告.md`.

## Core Engine

- The heroine knows or discovers that society expects her to become the villainess.
- Each episode should contain one visible flag: engagement event, school/social scene, heroine-route encounter, accusation, death/exile clue, or route correction.
- Agency comes from changing behavior, making allies, refusing scripted cruelty, or exploiting etiquette and memory.
- Romance often works best with a non-obvious male lead: second prince, duke, knight, mage, older political partner, or someone outside the original heroine's route.
- The payoff is not "she knows everything"; it is the gap between known route and changed human relationships.

## Six-Episode Season

1. Awakening at a bad flag: heroine realizes the original route and changes one immediate action.
2. Social deviation: a tea party, academy, salon, or engagement scene goes differently.
3. Route pressure: the original heroine, prince, family, or system of rumors tries to restore the old role.
4. Hidden ally: the male lead notices the heroine's changed conduct and protects a concrete choice.
5. Flag inversion: an accusation meant to condemn her reveals another person's manipulation.
6. New route declared: she survives the expected ruin and chooses a future not in the original story.

## Required Payoffs

- Original-role pressure.
- Flag recognition.
- Changed action.
- Social ripple.
- Romance with someone who recognizes the changed heroine.
- New-route uncertainty.

## Avoid

- Long explanation of the original game without a scene-level flag.
- Making every event easy because the heroine remembers it.
- Turning the heroine into a passive observer of route events.
