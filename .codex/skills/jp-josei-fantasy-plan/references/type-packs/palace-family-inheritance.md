# Type Pack: Palace / Family / Inheritance

Use for 王宮, palace intrigue, family coldness, inheritance, disinheritance, stepmother/stepchildren, divorce, mature remarriage, palace office work, and domestic political pressure.

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as structural references: family ledger, inheritance rule, palace office competence, child/stepfamily bond, and public legal reversal. If available, read `对标/palace-family-inheritance/{work}/拆文报告.md`.

## Core Engine

- The wound is domestic and social: family neglect, stolen inheritance, illegitimate accusation, childlessness, remarriage stigma, stepfamily conflict, or palace labor exploitation.
- The proof object is often a will, ledger, birth record, tax file, marriage contract, household account, adoption document, office memo, or witness.
- The heroine's agency is practical: administration, child care, negotiation, etiquette, household reform, bookkeeping, legal memory, or emotional steadiness.
- Romance may be lower heat and higher trust: a widower, duke, official, knight captain, or noble who sees her competence and dignity.
- The payoff repairs family position, legal status, or chosen-family belonging.

## Six-Episode Season

1. Family/palace wound: heroine is cast aside, overworked, blamed, divorced, or denied inheritance.
2. Record seed: she finds or preserves a document, ledger, child clue, or household contradiction.
3. New household/palace role: she enters a place where her overlooked competence matters.
4. Domestic pressure: old family, palace faction, step-relative, or inheritance rival attacks her standing.
5. Legal/social inquiry: records, children, staff, or officials reveal the truth.
6. Chosen family: legal status changes and the romance/family bond becomes publicly secure.

## Required Payoffs

- Domestic wound.
- Record or legal object.
- Practical competence.
- Family/palace witness.
- Status correction.
- Chosen-family warmth.

## Avoid

- Treating family abuse as endless suffering with no procedural progress.
- Legal reversals without documents or witnesses.
- Stepchildren as props without changing the heroine's choices.
