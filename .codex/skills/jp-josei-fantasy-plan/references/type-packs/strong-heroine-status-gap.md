# Type Pack: Strong Heroine / Status Gap / Exile Action Romance

Use for 強いヒロイン, sword/magic heroine, status difference, frontier action, exile survival, noble lady who fights, and romance with social gap.

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as structural references: action proof, status gap pressure, public misread, male-lead respect, and social correction. If available, read `对标/strong-heroine-status-gap/{work}/拆文报告.md`.

## Core Engine

- The heroine is physically, magically, administratively, or morally strong, but society reads that strength as improper, low-born, frightening, or unfeminine.
- Each episode should give her a real action or decision under pressure.
- The status gap must matter through etiquette, marriage law, class prejudice, military rank, court seating, inheritance, or rumors.
- Romance grows because the male lead respects capability without trying to shrink her.
- The payoff is not only defeating an enemy; it is society being forced to re-evaluate what kind of woman may be loved and honored.

## Six-Episode Season

1. Misread strength: heroine is mocked, exiled, or rejected for the very ability that will save someone.
2. First action proof: she solves a danger, battle, territory, or negotiation problem.
3. Status pressure: class, rank, court, or family tries to make her ability shameful.
4. Respect scene: male lead supports her action publicly or gives her proper authority.
5. Larger threat: her strength becomes necessary where etiquette alone fails.
6. Honor restored: she keeps her power and gains love/status without becoming smaller.

## Required Payoffs

- Visible heroine action.
- Status/class pressure.
- Risk or injury/cost.
- Male-lead respect for capability.
- Public re-evaluation.

## Avoid

- Action that removes romance and social consequence.
- "Strong heroine" who only talks sharply but makes no costly choice.
- Status gap that is solved by one declaration.
- A battle or competence climax that leaves the adult partnership as an optional epilogue reward.
