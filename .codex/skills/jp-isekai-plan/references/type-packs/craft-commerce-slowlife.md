# Type Pack: Craft / Commerce / Slow Life

Use for production, crafting, appraisal, repair, cooking, alchemy, shop, merchant, farming, slow life, base comfort, or "I just want peace but trouble keeps coming."

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as functional references: practical item value, guild/merchant pressure, recipe/material loop, public proof, comfort reward. If available, read `对标/craft-commerce-slowlife/{work}/拆文报告.md`.

## Core Engine

- Slow life is not "nothing happens"; it is a desired state interrupted by value, demand, jealousy, monsters, nobles, guilds, or supply limits.
- Every comfort scene must create, spend, protect, or reveal value.
- The protagonist's craft should have clear inputs, process, output, customer, and consequence. A hard limitation or personal price is optional.
- Default to a benefit-first ability: better quality, faster production, lower waste, new uses, safer work, higher profit, stronger base, or wider comfort. Let usefulness attract demand, rivals, supply pressure, protection duties, or political attention instead of punishing the protagonist for using the skill.

## Six-Episode Season

1. First product: protagonist makes/repairs/appraises/cooks one useful thing under pressure.
2. First customer: guild, merchant, village, or party pays and reacts.
3. Supply problem: material, tool, monster, weather, or regulation blocks production.
4. Public proof: a rival, noble, or guild sees the item's value.
5. Demand crisis: success attracts trouble, theft, bulk order, or monster resource problem.
6. Stable base: protagonist secures shop/farm/workshop/patron; next hook is a bigger order or dangerous material.

## Required Payoffs

- Concrete product or service.
- Input/process/output.
- Customer/witness reaction.
- Money/status/base change.
- Trouble caused by usefulness.

## Avoid

- Pure comfort scenes with no value movement.
- Replacing pressure with endless meals.
- Letting herbs/shopkeeping become the default for battle or OP premises.
- Turning every craft, farming, cooking, appraisal, or two-world ability into backlash, debt, future repayment, loss of control, or self-harm.
