# Type Pack: Secret Society / Subordinate Snowball

Use for secret organization, subordinate operation, faction snowball, hidden boss, accidental ruler, "my vague order became a genius plan", or protagonist managing overpowered followers.

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as functional references: vague command, subordinate over-interpretation, enemy myth inflation, organization growth, protagonist damage control. If available, read `对标/secret-society-subordinates/{work}/拆文报告.md`.

## Core Engine

- The protagonist's words/actions are interpreted as deeper than intended, or their actual plan is amplified by capable subordinates.
- The payoff comes from contrast: protagonist's calm/ordinary intent vs the faction's terrifying execution.
- Tension comes from keeping the organization hidden, controlling escalation, protecting followers, and not being trapped by the myth.

## Six-Episode Season

1. First follower: protagonist saves/commands someone who interprets them as a hidden master.
2. First mission: a small instruction becomes a successful operation.
3. Myth inflation: enemies assign a scary name or conspiracy to the group.
4. Internal pressure: subordinate loyalty, rivalry, or overreach creates a problem.
5. Public shadow: organization defeats a visible threat without revealing the protagonist.
6. Faction lock: the group gains a base, symbol, enemy, or title; next hook is a larger power investigating.

## Required Payoffs

- Subordinate competence.
- Misread or over-interpretation.
- Operation result.
- Enemy/public myth.
- Protagonist control problem.

## Avoid

- Subordinates with identical voices.
- Organization wins that need no protagonist decision.
- Copying known secret society names, rituals, uniforms, or scenes.
