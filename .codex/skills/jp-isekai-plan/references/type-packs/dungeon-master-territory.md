# Type Pack: Dungeon Master / Territory Management

Use for dungeon master, territory, base building, town management, traps, floor design, monster placement, resource defense, guild branch, or domain growth.

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as functional references: floor rule, resource loop, invasion defense, town/dungeon dual face, subordinate placement. If available, read `对标/dungeon-master-territory/{work}/拆文报告.md`.

## Core Engine

- The protagonist wins by designing a system: layout, traps, economy, monster ecology, defense, labor, and information flow.
- The payoff is "the enemy entered my rules without knowing it."
- Growth is visible as floor, room, resource, population, defense, tax/trade, guild access, or reputation.

## Six-Episode Season

1. Claim the base: protagonist gains a broken dungeon, village, ruin, or domain with one hidden advantage.
2. First rule: installs one floor/trap/resource rule that beats a small threat.
3. Economy loop: resource, monster drop, town trade, or guild traffic starts.
4. Rival probe: bandits, adventurers, noble, or monster faction tests the base.
5. Defense reveal: layered traps/subordinates/resources create a decisive reversal.
6. Territory recognized: base gains legal/social/fear status; next hook is larger invasion or alliance.

## Required Payoffs

- Map or space rule.
- Resource loop.
- Enemy misread.
- Defense or trap payoff.
- Territory/status consequence.

## Avoid

- Pure spreadsheet management with no scene pressure.
- Base growth without enemies or resource limits.
- Copying known dungeon layouts or factions.
