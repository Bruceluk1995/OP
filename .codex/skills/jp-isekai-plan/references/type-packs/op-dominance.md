# Type Pack: OP Dominance / Ruler Fantasy

Use for 龙傲天, 主人公最強, 开局无敌, hidden boss, demon lord, ruler, kingdom domination, "骨王 vibe", or any premise where the protagonist is already overwhelming.

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as functional references. Extract: enemy misread, protagonist restraint, subordinate reaction, public myth growth, territory/faction consequence. Do not copy named characters, organizations, undead/kingdom setup, scenes, titles, dialogue, or detailed plot order.

If a project has `对标/op-dominance/{work}/拆文报告.md`, read it before planning. Otherwise use this pack as the fallback.

## Core Engine

- The protagonist is not trying to become strong; they are managing the consequences of already being strong.
- Tension comes from incomplete information, public identity, collateral damage, political cost, subordinate over-interpretation, or not wanting to expose the full ceiling.
- The reveal should be controlled, not random. The protagonist chooses how much power to show.

## Six-Episode Season

1. Hidden arrival: local insult or threat underestimates the protagonist; one restrained demonstration changes a small power map.
2. Subordinate/faction contact: a loyal subordinate, local official, monster clan, or guild faction misreads the protagonist as a ruler-level figure.
3. Territory/resource gain: the protagonist secures a base, dungeon, village, relic, or trade route without showing full power.
4. Larger enemy probe: a noble, church, army, demon clan, or rival guild tests the myth and loses a proxy.
5. Controlled catastrophe: the protagonist gives one command or uses one spell/skill that rewrites the battlefield or negotiation.
6. Public myth lock: the world forms a new name/title/fear around the protagonist; next season hook is a kingdom-level response.

## Required Payoffs

- Enemy/world misread.
- Restrained decision.
- Overwhelming reveal or command.
- Witness/subordinate reaction.
- Reputation, territory, faction, or diplomatic consequence.

## Avoid

- Empty instant wins with no consequence.
- Randomly weakening the protagonist to create false tension.
- Copying famous OP ruler works' cast, court, guild names, throne scenes, or exact setup.
