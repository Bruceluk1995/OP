# Audience Pack: Japan

Use for Japanese-language scripts and Japan-facing angles. The viewer is curious, historically literate enough to know major world events, and sensitive to lectures that sound like imported Chinese internet geopolitics.

## Native Anxiety Points

- Island geography, sea lanes, resource and energy dependence.
- Aging society, low growth, social trust, bureaucracy, local communities.
- US alliance, constitutional constraints, postwar memory, regional security.
- Company, school, seniority, paperwork, consensus, and risk avoidance.

## Opening Objects

- Train timetable, convenience store shelf, electricity bill, city hall window, school club, company meeting, disaster drill, island map, imported fuel route.

## Rhetorical Style

- Use calm `です/ます` narration with sharp turns.
- Avoid triumphalism and imported slang.
- Let irony be dry, not loud.
- When explaining Western or global history, connect it to Japan through geography, trade, institutions, or everyday cost rather than "Japan should learn X".

## Good Final Thesis Shapes

- "The problem was not whether they were brave. It was whether the system could pay the bill."
- "An island country sees this first as security, then as price, and finally as daily life."
- "The strange choice was not strange once the exit had already disappeared."

## Avoid

- Chinese historical analogies as the main explanation.
- Treating Japan only as victim, student, or side character.
- Long Chinese-style punchlines that do not work in Japanese.
