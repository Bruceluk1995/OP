# Audience Pack: United States

Use for US-facing English scripts and topics where American viewers are the primary audience.

## Native Anxiety Points

- Federal versus state power, courts, constitution, rights language.
- Markets, taxes, inflation, debt, jobs, immigration, policing, healthcare, education.
- Military reach, alliances, credibility, logistics, shipping, energy, and empire without calling it empire.
- Individual freedom versus large institutions.

## Opening Objects

- Tax bill, gas price, court ruling, school board, highway, military base, grocery receipt, insurance form, border crossing, port crane, campaign rally.

## Rhetorical Style

- Direct, conversational, and evidence-led.
- Use dry punchlines after the mechanism is clear.
- Translate geopolitics into bills, supply chains, legal authority, and incentives.
- Avoid sounding like foreign scolding. The point is to explain why the system behaves as it does.

## Good Final Thesis Shapes

- "The issue was not whether America could win the fight. It was whether it could keep paying for the room after everyone else entered it."
- "That sounds like ideology. In practice, it was a logistics problem with a flag on top."
- "The system did not malfunction. It selected for this behavior."

## Avoid

- Anti-American ranting without mechanism.
- Treating the US as one unified mind.
- Using Chinese platform memes or translated slogans.
