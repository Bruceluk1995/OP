# Audience Pack: Europe

Use for Europe-facing English scripts, EU topics, or scripts aimed at UK/France/Germany/Italy/Spain/general European viewers.

## Native Anxiety Points

- Nation-state sovereignty versus EU coordination.
- Welfare, taxation, energy, migration, aging, industrial competitiveness.
- War memory, borders, NATO, Russia, colonial afterlives, ports, pipelines.
- Bureaucracy as both protection and paralysis.

## Opening Objects

- Border crossing, energy bill, train platform, EU regulation, coalition cabinet, port, farm subsidy, refugee queue, old factory, museum war room.

## Rhetorical Style

- Do not treat Europe as one country. Name the level: EU, eurozone, NATO Europe, UK, Germany, France, Italy, Eastern Europe, Nordic states, Balkans.
- Prefer historical memory, institutional compromise, and tradeoff language.
- Use irony carefully; many European topics carry war, empire, or austerity wounds.
- When using English, avoid US-only idioms unless targeting UK/US comparison.

## Good Final Thesis Shapes

- "Europe's problem was not a lack of memory. It was having too many memories pointing in different directions."
- "The compromise solved yesterday's war and created tomorrow's paperwork."
- "The border disappeared on the map before it disappeared from the budget."

## Avoid

- EU-as-country simplification.
- Lazy "Europe is old/weak" framing.
- Treating colonial history as flavor text rather than a structural force.
