# Topic Pack: Institutions / Economy / Power

Use for tax, money printing, prisons, corruption, salt/private trade, ports, bureaucracy, public finance, banking, labor, class, state capacity, and why systems reward apparently bad behavior.

## Core Engine

- Start with a common-sense question: why collect taxes if money can be printed; why bureaucracy grows; why corruption clusters; why institutions punish the wrong people.
- Convert morality into mechanism: information, enforcement cost, trust, accounting, legal authority, debt, labor discipline, and career incentives.
- Show why the "easy fix" creates a worse hidden cost.
- End by naming the institution's selection pressure.

## Best Openings

- A bill, tax form, bank counter, payroll, prison workshop, customs gate, or office meeting.
- A modern workplace analogy that explains an old institution.
- A small absurd rule that reveals the whole system.

## Required Payoffs

- One concrete transaction.
- One enforcement problem.
- One incentive contradiction.
- One institutional consequence.

## Avoid

- Abstract "capitalism/socialism/bureaucracy bad" essays.
- Financial claims without dates or sources.
- Overusing Chinese imperial analogies for non-Chinese audiences.
