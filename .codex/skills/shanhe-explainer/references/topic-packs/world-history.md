# Topic Pack: World History

Use for foreign history, empires, revolutions, succession crises, war turning points, colonial systems, trade routes, modernization, state collapse, and "why did this civilization/country choose that path" questions.

## Core Engine

- Start from a paradox: advantage did not become victory, weakness became survival, or a "stupid" decision was rational under constraints.
- Move from event to structure: geography, food, manpower, finance, succession, technology, religion, trade, or legitimacy.
- Use one comparison to prove the mechanism, not to decorate chronology.
- End with the bill that came due: the old advantage became a trap, or the ignored constraint finally became visible.

## Best Openings

- A date/place scene with one absurd detail.
- A map problem: river, sea lane, mountain, border, port, capital, colony.
- A succession or legitimacy problem: who can command, tax, inherit, or claim the future.
- A "they should have won" opening that immediately shows why the obvious answer is incomplete.

## Required Payoffs

- Explain why the obvious moral answer is insufficient.
- Name the decisive constraint.
- Show how at least two actors were trapped by that constraint.
- Give the audience one reusable historical lens.

## Avoid

- Dynasty-by-dynasty chronology.
- Assuming Chinese history is the default comparison.
- Treating Europe, the Islamic world, Africa, India, or Latin America as background for great-power stories only.
