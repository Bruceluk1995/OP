# Topic Pack: Japan History / Society

Use for Japan-focused history, society, economy, institutions, postwar order, aging, energy dependence, bureaucracy, regional decline, education, work culture, public finance, disaster governance, and island-state geopolitics.

## Core Engine

- Start from a familiar Japanese object or anxiety: train timetable, city hall form, electricity bill, company meeting, school rule, empty shopping street, convenience store shelf, disaster drill, port map, or imported fuel route.
- Convert "Japanese uniqueness" into mechanism: island geography, resource dependence, bureaucratic coordination, social trust, demographic aging, postwar security order, local finance, land use, labor norms, or education incentives.
- Avoid pure nostalgia. The question is not "Japan used to be great" or "Japan is doomed"; it is which old solution became today's constraint.
- Use one foreign comparison only when it clarifies Japan's mechanism. Do not let Japan become a side example in its own lane.

## Best Openings

- A daily scene that Japanese viewers immediately recognize: station delay notice, municipal counter, electricity price, neighborhood association, school entrance exam, company transfer order.
- A historical hinge: Meiji state-building, postwar growth, Plaza Accord, bubble collapse, 1995, Heisei stagnation, demographic turn, energy shock, alliance dilemma.
- A map problem: islands, sea lanes, ports, mountains, disaster zones, rural depopulation, US bases, nearby great powers.

## Required Payoffs

- One Japan-native anxiety.
- One structural mechanism beyond culture talk.
- One tradeoff: safety versus flexibility, consensus versus speed, stability versus growth, autonomy versus alliance, local survival versus national efficiency.
- One clear final judgment that Japanese viewers can apply to a current question.

## Title Shapes

- `なぜ日本はXを変えられないのか`
- `日本の強みは、いつから弱点になったのか`
- `これは根性論ではなく、制度の問題です`
- `島国日本が避けられなかった請求書`

## Avoid

- "Japanese people are just..." explanations.
- Imported Chinese internet judgments.
- Treating all Japanese choices as cowardice, decline, or moral failure.
- Explaining Japan only through China, the US, or Europe.
