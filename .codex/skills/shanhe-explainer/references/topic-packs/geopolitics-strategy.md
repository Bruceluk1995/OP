# Topic Pack: Geopolitics / Strategy

Use for alliances, sea power, land power, sanctions, war plans, military logistics, great-power competition, global order, chokepoints, proxy conflict, and "why does a country have to act this way" questions.

## Core Engine

- The real protagonist is often geography plus logistics, not a single leader.
- Convert ideology into incentives: security, ports, resources, buffers, credibility, supply chains, domestic audience, and alliance cost.
- Separate "can win a fight" from "can afford the order after the fight".
- Treat escalation, face, deterrence, and retreat as cost structures.

## Best Openings

- A map/chokepoint image: strait, border, island chain, pipeline, port, bridge, base.
- A bill image: fuel, shipping insurance, ammunition, debt, reconstruction, alliance subsidy.
- A public boast that collapses when logistics enters the room.
- A small country forcing a large country to reveal its real constraint.

## Required Payoffs

- Security dilemma.
- Geography or supply-chain constraint.
- Alliance or credibility cost.
- A final judgment that distinguishes winning an event from maintaining a system.

## Avoid

- Pure camp cheering.
- "Country X is evil/stupid" as a substitute for incentives.
- Current claims without verification.
- Translating Chinese internet geopolitics jokes directly for Japanese, US, or European viewers.
