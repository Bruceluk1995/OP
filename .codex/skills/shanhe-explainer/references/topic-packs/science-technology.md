# Topic Pack: Science / Technology / Civilization

Use for science history, AI, math, climate, physics, energy, infrastructure, agriculture, medicine, computing, and "why does this technical thing matter to ordinary people" questions.

## Core Engine

- Begin with an everyday observation or counterintuitive number.
- Explain the action before the label: show what the model, machine, equation, climate process, or technology does.
- Connect the technical mechanism to institutions: industry, state power, labor, war, health, food, energy, or information.
- End with a change in human options, not just a fact.

## Best Openings

- Heat, food, phone, train, classroom, hospital, electricity bill, search box, weather map.
- A wrong intuitive answer: "it feels hotter", "AI is just autocomplete", "math is abstract", "technology always makes things cheaper".
- A scale jump: household object -> city -> country -> planet.

## Required Payoffs

- One concrete mechanism.
- One analogy that does not distort the science.
- One institutional or historical consequence.
- One clear limit: what the explanation does not prove.

## Avoid

- Fake precision.
- Unsupported medical, climate, finance, or AI claims.
- Turning science into motivational prose.
