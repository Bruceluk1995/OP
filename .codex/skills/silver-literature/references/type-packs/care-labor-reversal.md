# Type Pack: Care Labor Reversal

Use for 介護労働, unpaid care, daughter-in-law caregiving, spouse care, elder-care burden, care records, and family members discarding the caregiver after years of labor.

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as structural references: care burden, proof record, family handoff, public confrontation, and late-life recovery. If available, read `对标/care-labor-reversal/{work}/拆文报告.md`.

## Core Engine

- The protagonist has performed years of invisible care.
- The family calls the labor natural duty, then discards, insults, or financially exploits the caregiver.
- The proof is concrete: care diary, medicine schedule, hospital record, facility invoice, neighbor/nurse testimony, meal log, payment ledger.
- The reversal forces the family to face the labor they mocked.
- Recovery must show the protagonist gaining time, room, health, money, or chosen family.

## Six-Episode Season

1. Care insult: family says care was "nothing" or demands more.
2. Old debt: routines and records reveal the true labor load.
3. False compliance: protagonist appears to accept divorce, eviction, or continued care.
4. Proof handoff: diary/invoice/witness enters a family or legal venue.
5. Responsibility rebound: the family receives the care burden or public accountability.
6. Quiet release: protagonist leaves, rests, works, moves, or reconnects with someone kind.

## Required Payoffs

- Care labor shown through records/routines.
- Family contempt.
- Proof object.
- Responsibility rebound.
- Protagonist boundary.
- Late-life rest or restart.

## Avoid

- Endless suffering without procedural movement.
- Care work described only as vague kindness.
- Punishment that does not connect to care responsibility.
