# Type Pack: Funeral / Will / Posthumous Truth

Use for 葬式, 遺言, 終活, inheritance reveal, final letter, memorial object, insurance, old photo, and the deceased protecting or exposing someone after death.

## Benchmark Protocol

Use hot works only as structural references: funeral insult, will reading, final letter, hidden witness, grief plus justice. If available, read `对标/funeral-will-posthumous-truth/{work}/拆文报告.md`.

## Core Engine

- Death or end-of-life preparation exposes the family's real face.
- Someone tries to erase the protagonist from grief, inheritance, memory, or family status.
- Proof comes from will, final letter, recording, old photo, insurance, land deed, bank seal, funeral arrangement, or end-of-life note.
- The reversal should carry grief, not only money.
- Recovery honors the deceased while freeing the living protagonist.

## Six-Episode Season

1. Funeral wound: protagonist is excluded, insulted, or used at a funeral/terminal moment.
2. Old promise: memory/object shows what the deceased actually knew.
3. Greed overreach: family rushes money, house, keepsake, or blame.
4. Will/letter reveal: final proof changes the meaning of the past.
5. Public consequence: inheritance, apology, disinheritance, or exposed betrayal.
6. Quiet vow: protagonist protects the promise and starts again.

## Required Payoffs

- Death/終活 pressure.
- Memorial proof object.
- Greed or exclusion.
- Public reading/reveal.
- Emotional justice.
- Promise-preserving ending.

## Avoid

- Treating death as a cheap trick.
- Inheritance math without grief.
- Copying will-reading scenes too directly.
