# Silver Literature Patterns

Use these as structural patterns, not as claims about live rankings. Browse when the user asks what is popular now or provides fresh channels/videos.

When no strong project benchmark exists, follow `dynamic-benchmarking.md` before relying on this snapshot. These patterns are scaffolds, not a substitute for project拆文, live public metadata, or project-local subtype notes.

## Market Signals

- Silver-life stories use high-retention revenge mechanics, but the emotional core is dignity after long endurance.
- The strongest hooks combine everyday family realism with one sharp abnormal event: medical warning, divorce paper, property title, care diary, will, bank freeze, inheritance notice, pension theft, hidden recording, or facility invoice.
- Older protagonists often win because they own the ignored foundation: house, company history, family registry, care records, old promise, pension, inheritance, professional license, neighborhood trust, or social credibility.
- The audience payoff is not only punishment. It is the moment the protagonist stops being treated as furniture and becomes a person with rights, history, and a future.

## High-Retention Structure

Use a six-part structure for most silver-literature narration:

1. Opening bomb: begin with a concrete cruelty, document, warning, or missing object.
2. Accumulated debt: reveal years of care, money, silence, labor, or love through routines and records.
3. False compliance: the protagonist appears to accept divorce, eviction, demotion, abandonment, care duty, or money loss.
4. Evidence turn: a document, witness, professional identity, recording, will, diagnosis, or property title returns with new meaning.
5. Public consequence: the antagonist's private exploitation is exposed in a visible arena.
6. Late-life recovery: end with a small concrete recovery: room, meal, garden, train, job, hobby, grandchild, old friend, or gentle romance.

The key rhythm is not "abuse -> revenge" only. It is "old contribution ignored -> proof restores reality -> protagonist chooses the rest of life."

## Reliable Engines

- Care labor reversal: unpaid care is called natural duty until records, invoices, and witness testimony make the labor visible.
- Rights reversal: someone treats the house, pension, inheritance, or bank account as shared prey; legal ownership becomes emotional boundary.
- Retirement money trap: a spouse or child waits for pension, severance, retirement bonus, or insurance, then discovers the protagonist already protected it.
- Medical proof: diagnosis, medicine, consent, nurse note, or injury pattern changes "family matter" into public accountability.
- Posthumous truth: funeral, will, letter, or memorial object lets the deceased protect the protagonist after death.
- Old merit reveal: the "ordinary old person" is founder, owner, expert, donor, witness, designer, former nurse, accountant, or trusted worker.
- Adult-child reckoning: the child who demanded money/care learns exactly what the parent had been carrying.
- Facility/housing pressure: care home, senior share house, old apartment, or family home forces a choice about safety and autonomy.
- Late-life romance: companionship restores future possibility, but does not erase legal/family consequences.

## Long-Episode Density

For a long narration episode around 15,000 characters, do not stretch the story with weather, room atmosphere, gentle memories, repeated loneliness, or moral commentary. Make the length come from more family/legal/financial/care state changes.

- When the user asks for YouTube/朗读 "完整正文", treat 14,500-16,500 Japanese characters as the default target unless another number is stated.
- Do not add a fixed state-change quota. Let the beat count follow the outline, emotional target, and silver-life rhythm.
- Any long stretch changes, prepares, or pays off at least one of: old labor/debt, proof, witness, legal status, money/property, care responsibility, housing, health risk, family relationship, public reputation, or protagonist choice.
- Every 2,500-3,000 characters lands proof movement, responsibility rebound, public pressure, visible consequence, or late-life recovery step.
- Sparse transitions only move time/place and should be brief.
- No scene may exist only for atmosphere, weather, seasonal memory, empty loneliness, generic kindness, quiet sadness, or elegant phrasing.

Paragraph test:

```text
If I delete this paragraph, what changes in the family/legal/financial/care situation?
```

If the answer is only "mood", "sympathy", "old memory", or "beautiful narration", cut it or attach it to a concrete object, witness, decision, record, or consequence.

## Satisfaction Points

Make each story deliver at least four of these payoffs:

- The "useless old person/housewife/caregiver" is revealed as owner, expert, donor, founder, witness, or the only person who kept the family alive.
- The protagonist was not swallowing humiliation; they were collecting proof, protecting someone, waiting for legal timing, or preserving dignity.
- Unpaid care, stolen money, emotional neglect, and property exploitation become visible as numbers, records, witnesses, or labor the family cannot replace.
- A calm line flips the relationship, such as "では、介護は今日からあなたにお返しします", "この家は私の名義です", or "離婚届はもう提出してあります".
- Hospital, lawyer office, company, funeral, family meeting, bank, facility, or police visit turns private arrogance into public consequence.
- After justice, the protagonist gains a key, room, job, hobby, meal, companion, grandchild recognition, or train ticket toward a second life.

Do not stack only revenge. Silver-literature payoff = justice plus dignity plus late-life possibility.

## Review Gates

Before delivery, verify:

- The protagonist is meaningfully middle-aged or older, or the story turns on elder-family pressure.
- The injustice is legible in the first 300 Japanese characters.
- The selected lane has its own visible engine.
- The reversal has a causal proof chain; punishment is not arbitrary.
- The story does not treat illness, disability, pregnancy, sexual harm, dementia, or elder abuse as cheap spectacle.
- The ending restores dignity through a concrete choice, not only revenge.
- Japanese prose sounds like narration for mature viewers: clear, emotionally direct, scene-based, and not Chinese webnovel-flavored.
