## Dense/Sparse Rules

- Dense beats are for humiliation, care/labor proof, legal/financial records, medical findings, witness testimony, protagonist boundary-setting, public reversal, and emotional recovery.
- Sparse beats are for room changes, time movement, routine explanation, and already-understood context. Compress them.
- A dense payoff beat should usually contain object, witness, choice, and consequence.
- Each beat must say "who does what" plus a function tag such as setup, old debt, proof, pressure, protagonist choice, witness, reversal, consequence, recovery, or hook.

## Lane Payoff Floor

When a plan names a subtype lane, every story/episode must carry that lane's engine at scene level:

- Care labor reversal: care diary, facility invoice, medicine schedule, family handoff, or responsibility rebound.
- Late-life divorce/retirement money: divorce paper, retirement bonus, pension, affair evidence, property division, or calm exit.
- Property/inheritance rights: deed, will, registry, bankbook, tax record, lawyer letter, or asset boundary.
- Hospital/medical neglect: diagnosis, consent form, nurse/doctor witness, medicine record, emergency call, or safety intervention.
- Funeral/will/posthumous truth: memorial object, final letter, will reading, insurance record, old photo, or deceased person's last protection.
- Hidden identity/old merit: license, company register, old client, craft proof, founder record, shareholder list, or professional witness.
- Adult-child reckoning: transfer record, unpaid care ledger, grandchild witness, family meeting, or parent-child boundary.
- Nursing home/facility: care plan, facility bill, resident witness, dementia memory object, safety check, or housing decision.
- Pension/bank/scam: passbook, pension notice, frozen card, scam call, bank counter, police report, or account transfer.
- Workplace retirement/reputation: retirement party insult, employee ID, client list, boardroom, union/legal record, or successor recognition.
- Second-life romance/remarriage: old promise, widow/widower boundary, family opposition, practical care, public choosing, or peaceful companion image.
- Neighbor/community/housing: neighborhood association, security footage, old house key, shared road/parking dispute, local witness, or community apology.

## Anti-Water Expansion

When the draft is short, expand only existing dense beats:

- split an accusation into claim, proof object, protagonist calculation, witness reaction, legal/social consequence, and next choice
- add a document, invoice, bankbook, key, medicine list, old photo, record, facility bill, or witness
- add protagonist agency with real cost
- make late-life recovery concrete through a room, meal, route, job, hobby, companion, health appointment, or family boundary

Do not expand by adding abstract pain, pretty weather, generic loneliness, repeated memories, moral summaries, or scenery.

## Delivery Gate

Before delivering:

- First sentence contains a concrete family/legal/financial/care bomb.
- The first 300 Japanese characters make the injustice legible.
- The selected lane's proof engine appears on page.
- Dense beats are visibly denser than transitions.
- At least one proof/status/care/money/reputation consequence lands on the page.
- The final paragraph points to recovery or next episode through a concrete object, witness, document, choice, or quiet life image.
- Count the saved prose. For YouTube/朗读完整正文, 14,500-16,500 Japanese characters is the default pass range. If it is below 14,500, expand only dense beats with new proof, witness, legal/financial/care movement, protagonist choice, public consequence, or recovery action. Do not pad with atmosphere or repeated emotion.
