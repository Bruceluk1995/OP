# Push Entertainment Gate

Use this for every anime-recap/push-narration draft before viewpoint approval and surface lint. Read `push-prompt-architecture.md` first. It rejects copy that is accurate, eventful, and easy to subtitle but still feels like a plot log, operating manual, or institutional report.

## What Counts as Interesting

`A fact changed` is necessary but not sufficient. An expanded event earns space only when it changes what the audience feels, wants, fears, judges, or waits to see.

Audit against the locked speaker/listener and audience emotional contracts. If a module completes a plot function but neither sounds like the selected speaker nor changes the listener's emotional pressure, it is not ready to expand.

A rule, pipe, contract, status panel, skill route, investigation result, or policy change counts only when it forces a person to choose, lose, expose, betray, refuse, risk, or change a relationship or public status. Technical state change without human collision is bridge material.

Before outlining, complete this for every event marked `expand`:

```text
who wants what right now:
who or what actively blocks it:
visible collision:
human price, status, or relationship at risk:
narrator's useful take:
memorable action or decisive exact line:
new audience question:
```

If the collision, human stake, and memorable action/line fields are empty, relabel the event `one-line bridge` or `cut`.

## Human-Heat Rules

- Explain a mechanism through a failure, threat, humiliation, irreversible object, choice, or exact line of speech, then return to the person's decision.
- If roughly 200-400 consecutive characters are dominated by system nouns and procedure verbs while nobody wants, blocks, chooses, refuses, loses, or speaks, presume flowchart failure.
- Named companions cannot function as interchangeable skill modules. Each important companion needs at least one specific desire, refusal, risk, choice, or line whose removal changes the plan or relationship.
- Human conflict cannot be paraphrased into one neutral voice from beginning to end. In a long conflict-driven script, preserve a few decisive exact lines when their wording changes power, allegiance, humiliation, danger, or choice. This is a voice heuristic, not a dialogue quota.
- Do not turn the script into a transcript. Report routine dialogue and quote only the words worth remembering.

Reject this procedure list:

```text
セラは優先順位を決め
リネは管を結び
ミアは所有印を消し
ベラが順番に点火した
```

Prefer a collision that makes the mechanism matter:

```text
ミアが管を一本拭いた瞬間、王立病院の灯りが全部消えた。
学院長は初めて笑った。
「続けろ。お前が一本消すたび、勇者より先に病人が死ぬ」
四人の目的は炉の破壊から、止めずに奪い返すことへ変わった。
```

## Payoff Curve

The title and central promise normally pay inside the final 15-25% of a closed one-shot. This is a diagnostic, not a mandatory formula.

- If the advertised promise pays earlier, a stronger and genuinely different promise must remain alive.
- After the final central payoff, default to no more than roughly 10-15% for one consequence beat and one ending proof.
- A new mechanism explanation, policy list, second test, second climax, or extended safe-return procedure after the final payoff is a post-payoff tail, not closure.
- A closed story should feel finished at its strongest earned consequence, not after every rule has been administratively settled.

Record the title-promise payoff position and post-final-payoff share against non-whitespace body characters. Missing the range may be justified only by naming the stronger later promise and its payoff.

## Voice and Oral Shape

- Draft natural spoken sentences and short oral paragraphs first. Split them for subtitles only after the entertainment and viewpoint gates pass.
- Do not add causal connectors, contrast words, quotations, or line breaks to hit a ratio.
- Read without seeing line breaks. The subject, causal chain, human conflict, and punch lines must remain understandable by audio alone.
- A median spoken-unit length at or below 12 characters combined with more than 80 spoken units per 1,000 non-whitespace characters is an over-fragmentation candidate. It requires recombining clauses; it is not proof of energy.
- In first person, every expanded module needs a selection, judgment, misunderstanding, refusal, or choice that only this narrator would make. If a 200-400-character module survives replacement by a neutral camera, it fails the module voice-swap test.
- In third person, every expanded module needs deliberate narrator selection or contrast. Neutral event inventory fails even when labels and knowledge boundaries are correct.

## Adversarial Release Read

Read the saved body without outline, metrics, or author explanation and answer with exact anchors:

```text
first place entertainment dies:
first place the locked speaker/listener voice disappears:
first expanded module whose emotional job does not change audience pressure:
first 200-400-character flowchart/procedure run:
first module without human collision:
first module where narrator voice can be swapped or turns neutral:
first important character used only as a function/module:
title/main promise payoff position:
stronger promise after it, or none:
post-final-payoff share:
five memorable lines, choices, or actions after the opening:
audio-only verdict:
15% cut test — what can disappear without reducing fun or emotion:
verdict and earliest production stage to redo:
```

`ready for user read` is forbidden when the evidence relies on the opening alone, when the middle is a procedure chain, when the final payoff is followed by a long administrative tail, or when the editor cannot name memorable human choices and voices after the opening.
