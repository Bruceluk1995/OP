# Prose Style Guide

Use this before drafting audience-facing Japanese prose.

## Narrative Shape

- Open with an emotional or social hook within the first 300-500 Japanese characters.
- Keep the heroine close to the reader: shame, restraint, calculation, fear, anger, relief, and hope should be legible.
- Show status through behavior: seating order, titles, forms of address, witnesses, etiquette, documents, seals, rumors, and servants.
- Let romance grow through action. A male lead who remembers a detail, believes evidence, shields her reputation, or offers fair terms is stronger than generic praise.
- Keep paragraphs web-readable. Use dialogue and internal reaction to vary pace.

## Female-Reader Fantasy Romance Priorities

- Dignity recovery matters as much as plot victory.
- The heroine needs agency even when she receives rescue.
- `溺愛` should feel specific: he values her mind, work, courage, kindness, or pain that others ignored.
- `ざまぁ` should satisfy without making the heroine seem needlessly cruel.
- A happy-ending direction should remain visible even during painful family abuse or public humiliation.

## Japanese Prose Rules

- Use Japanese quotation marks and punctuation for Japanese prose.
- Prefer natural `私` first person or close third person. Avoid mixing Chinese sentence order into Japanese.
- Do not explain the tag labels inside the prose.
- Do not write synopsis-like paragraphs when the task asks for fiction. Convert summary into scene, action, dialogue, and internal response.
- Avoid stacked system panels and game-log formatting unless explicitly requested.

## Common Scene Beats

- Ballroom annulment: witnesses, fiance's accusation, heroine's composure, one impossible detail, male lead's intervention.
- Family coldness: dinner-table neglect, document theft, servant rumor, heroine quietly preserving evidence.
- Contract marriage: terms, separate rooms, awkward courtesy, first small act of care.
- Cursed noble: feared mansion, visible curse symptom, heroine notices pain not monstrosity.
- Frontier slow life: practical labor, food or medicine, local gratitude, distant court threat.
