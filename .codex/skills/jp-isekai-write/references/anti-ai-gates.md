# JP Isekai Anti-AI Gates

Use this after the episode blueprint and again after drafting. It adapts the generic `story-deslop` gates to Japanese male-audience isekai prose.

Goal: remove AI texture without destroying Japanese web-novel readability, first-person humor, RPG fantasy vocabulary, or push-narration retention.

Hard style rule: reject literary prose. Do not keep long environment description or long psychology description; rewrite them into colloquial action, dialogue, choice, cost, reward, or consequence.

## Gate 0: Protect Plot Function

Do not rewrite for prettiness. Keep:

- plot-bomb opening
- previous-episode bridge
- skill rule
- item, monster, guild, money, rank, debt, reward, and location continuity
- protagonist agency and comic complaint
- next-episode hook

Only change how a line says the thing. Do not invent new plot, new rules, or new relationships during deslop.

## Gate A: Delete Generic Texture

Ban or heavily restrict generic lines that could fit any story:

- `運命`, `宿命`, `光`, `静寂`, `世界が変わる`, `新しい人生`, `胸が締め付けられる`, `言葉を失う`, `温かさが広がる`
- repeated sighing, trembling, smiling, heart-tightening, light/silence imagery
- atmosphere paragraphs with no object, price, wound, witness, monster, rule, or action

Replace with isekai-specific anchors:

- guild tag, quest sheet, copper/silver/gold coins, monster part, broken tool, rented room, food, debt note, familiar collar, spell mark, rank card
- a practical action: count coins, hide a monster claw, test a skill on a cheap object, argue with guild staff, patch equipment

## Gate B: Break Stock Sentences

Rewrite:

- `それはXではなくYだった` when it only sounds neat.
- `まるで世界が...` metaphors.
- `その瞬間、すべてが...` summary lines.
- complete explanatory sentences that state the system rule before the scene proves it.
- long balanced paragraphs that explain crisis, feeling, and theme in one polished block.

Prefer:

- action first, rule second
- a visible failed attempt before explanation
- one practical protagonist aside after the serious beat

## Gate C: Externalize Emotion

Do not say the protagonist is moved, afraid, hopeful, or lonely unless the line also changes a scene.

Use body/object behavior:

- count coins twice and still be short
- grip the guild tag until the edge bites the palm
- look at the rent note before answering
- move the familiar behind his boot
- test the skill on a dented pan lid instead of making a heroic speech

## Gate D: Fix Rhythm And Density

- Dense beats get scene-level action, dialogue, witness reaction, and consequence.
- Sparse beats get one or two sentences.
- Do not make every paragraph the same length.
- Do not create a chain of tiny one-sentence paragraphs unless the scene is a pressure beat.
- In a 12,000-character episode, do not use long quiet stretches as filler. Any long stretch must change, prepare, or pay off information, resource, risk, status, relationship, skill understanding, enemy pressure, objective, or next decision.
- Delete paragraphs that only describe atmosphere, scenery, awe, loneliness, silence, light, destiny, or vague feelings without changing the state above.
- If the Codex AI reread notices short-sentence stutter, merge short fragments into a fuller action chain.
- If it reports `long-paragraph`, split by new action, object, line of dialogue, or information turn.

## Gate D2: No Pretty Dead Text

For each suspect paragraph, ask:

```text
What changed because this paragraph exists?
```

Keep it only if the answer names a concrete change: a price is learned, a skill behaves differently, a witness reacts, a monster/tool/guild rule changes the plan, money/status/risk moves, or the protagonist makes a choice. If the answer is only "atmosphere", "beauty", "loneliness", "tension", or "style", cut it or rewrite it around an object/action/consequence.

## Gate E: Make Dialogue Human

Male-audience isekai dialogue can be blunt, practical, and funny.

- Avoid everyone speaking in polished full explanations.
- Guild staff, nobles, villagers, adventurers, and protagonist should not sound the same.
- Replace exposition dialogue with conflict: denial, price, condition, threat, misunderstanding, or paperwork.
- Use short protagonist reaction lines when they sharpen the beat: rent, food, tools, debt, risk, reward.

## Gate F: End On Object Or Choice

Do not end with abstract growth, destiny, or "a new life begins."

End with:

- a quest sheet stamped with a dangerous rank
- a monster part that should not move
- a coin reward that is too high or too low
- a familiar reacting to an unseen enemy
- a guild rule that changes the next action
- the protagonist choosing between safety and profit

## Gate G: Remove Author Explanation

Delete or convert:

- `彼はまだ知らなかった`
- `これが後に...`
- `つまり...`
- `それは大きな意味を持っていた`
- narrator explanations of what the reader should feel

Convert them into what the protagonist can see, hear, touch, count, smell, or misunderstand now.

## Codex AI File Pass

When the episode is saved to a file, Codex must reread the saved file itself before delivery. Do not rely on a mechanical gate. Check for AI texture, production metadata leakage, Chinese leakage, repeated rhythm, and dead paragraphs by reasoning over the scene function and surrounding context.

## Final Self-Check

- Did I remove abstract filler without deleting plot function?
- Does every emotional line have an object, action, dialogue, body response, or consequence?
- Does every long stretch in a long episode have a function beyond atmosphere, scenery, mood, or adjectives?
- Is the prose colloquial and direct rather than literary, ornate, or psychology-heavy?
- Are dense beats visibly denser than transitions?
- Does the ending hook point to a concrete next action?
- Did no engineering words, placeholders, or model refusal text leak into prose?
