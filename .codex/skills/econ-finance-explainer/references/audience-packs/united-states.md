# Audience Pack: United States

Use for US-oriented English economics/finance explainers.

## Native Anxiety Points

- Credit-card debt, student loans, mortgages, rent, healthcare bills.
- Stock-market retirement accounts, 401(k), inflation, Fed rates.
- Federal versus state policy, taxes, local services.
- Gig work, platforms, union bargaining, healthcare tied to jobs.
- Individual freedom versus institutional design.

## Opening Objects

- credit-card statement;
- student-loan email;
- mortgage-rate quote;
- grocery receipt;
- 401(k) app;
- insurance bill;
- gig-worker dashboard.

## Style

- Direct, concrete, and mechanism-first.
- Use "follow the cash flow" often as a mental frame.
- Avoid academic jargon unless immediately translated into a scene.

## Avoid

- Giving trade calls.
- Treating US institutions as universal.
- Importing Asian education or housing assumptions without explanation.
