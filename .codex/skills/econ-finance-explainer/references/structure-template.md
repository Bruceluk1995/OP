# Structure Template

Use this for outlines and full scripts.

## Standard Long Explainer Arc

### 1. Cold Open

Length: 8-12% of script.

Start with a daily money scene, a strange market promise, or a verified number. Do not open with textbook definitions.

Examples of opening objects:

- a salary notification;
- a credit-card bill;
- a mortgage reset notice;
- a train-station ad for investment products;
- an app showing "AI saved you three hours";
- a local government building a new road with borrowed money.

### 2. Audience Lock

Make the problem native to the audience:

- Japan: wages, yen weakness, aging, pensions, local decline, low rates, lifetime employment aftershocks, convenience-service expectations.
- United States: credit cards, student loans, mortgages, stock-market retirement accounts, healthcare bills, federal-state budgets.
- Europe: energy bills, welfare state pressure, ECB rates, housing shortage, EU rules, youth unemployment, aging and migration.
- Chinese-speaking: housing, education, local debt, platform work, social mobility, family savings, consumer finance.

### 3. Misconception Lock

State the popular explanation and refine it:

- "Most people think this is about laziness. That explains almost nothing."
- "The problem is not that people forgot how to save. It is that the game board changed."
- "If this were only a price problem, the solution would be simple. It is not."

### 4. Mechanism 1: Concrete Money Flow

Trace where money goes:

- household income and fixed costs;
- bank interest and loan terms;
- fees and commissions;
- platform cut and ad auction;
- tax, subsidies, or debt service;
- asset price and leverage.

### 5. Mechanism 2: Incentive And Power

Explain why actors keep doing it:

- the lender can resell risk;
- the platform owns traffic;
- the employer can reset wages;
- the government can roll debt forward;
- the investor is chasing yield;
- the consumer misreads probability.

### 6. Feedback Loop

Show how the mechanism scales:

- more credit raises prices, higher prices require more credit;
- more degrees raise the hiring bar, not the job skill;
- more AI tools lower unit price, forcing more output;
- more local borrowing creates projects that need more refinancing.

### 7. Comparison

Use one comparison only when it sharpens the mechanism:

- another country;
- an earlier crisis;
- a similar platform or market;
- a household-scale analogy.

### 8. Rule And Final Judgment

End with a concrete rule:

- "When the upside is advertised to you and the downside is contractually yours, you are not investing. You are absorbing risk."
- "Efficiency does not automatically become leisure. It becomes leisure only when workers have bargaining power."
- "Debt is not evil. But debt used to hide an income problem will return as a control problem."

## New User Intake

Ask in this order:

1. 观众主要在哪里？
   - 日本
   - 美国
   - 欧洲
   - 中文观众
   - 其他/国际
2. 你现在是哪种情况？
   - 我还没有题目，想先找财经/经济学选题
   - 我有一个关键词/热点，想包装成标题
   - 我已有题目，想先做讲解稿大纲
   - 我已有题目或大纲，想直接写完整稿
   - 我有视频/字幕，想拆解套路
3. If no topic yet, ask source:
   - Google Trends / market news trend
   - 常青财经选题，不追热点
   - 用户给关键词
4. Topic lane:
   - 家庭财务 / 普通人经济学
   - 宏观经济 / 利率 / 通胀
   - 金融市场 / IPO / 房地产 / 风险
   - 政府债务 / 税收 / 公共财政
   - 平台经济 / AI / 劳动与资本
   - 行为经济学 / 骗局 / 赌博和保险
5. Target language and length.

## Full Script Checks

Before delivery:

- The first 12 lines contain a scene, paradox, or number.
- Every 500-800 Japanese characters, 250-400 English words, or 500-800 Chinese characters introduces a new mechanism, comparison, or verified fact.
- The script answers "who pays, who gains, who carries risk."
- The ending gives a reusable rule, not only emotion.
- Current facts have source links or are marked as requiring verification.
- The length is not copied from the short corpus unless the user explicitly asked for a short clip.
- A complete Japanese or Chinese episode normally targets approximately 12,000 characters. If shorter, add a new mechanism, counterargument, household decision, policy tradeoff, or consequence only when needed; do not expand merely to cross a minimum.
