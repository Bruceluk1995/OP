# Third-Person Push Benchmark

Use this synthetic benchmark for anime recap/push narration. Learn functions, not wording.

## Reject: Third-Person Novel Scene Broken into Lines

```text
カイルは工房の扉を開けた
中には三人の兵士がいた
隊長は壊れた盾を指さした
カイルは盾へ近づいた
彼は修理できるか考えた
```

This acts out routine movement, repeats the name, head-hops, and delays the abnormal result. Short lines do not make it push narration.

## Reject: Neutral Plot Summary

```text
修理工のカイルは追放された
その後に砦へ行った
そこで壊れた門を直した
兵士たちは驚いた
最後に彼は評価された
```

This reports chronology without causal pressure, narrator stance, tactical choice, or a live question. Crowd reaction substitutes for proof.

## Target: Narrator-Led Third-Person Push

```text
廃棄寸前の門を選んだ修理工は
その場で勇者隊を追放された
ところが一時間後
無傷の聖剣では止められなかった魔王軍を
救ったのはその腐った門だった
修理工カイルに見えていたのは強度ではない
門が壊れるまで残り四十七分
だから彼は門を強くせず
敵の初撃と同時に壊れるよう梁を削った
```

The narrator leads with outcome, selects a practical contrast, compresses cause and result, and keeps the protagonist central without repeating his name.

## Stable-Label Test

- Introduce the full name once when it matters.
- Then omit the subject or rotate one clear role label such as `修理工`, `追放者`, or `少女`; do not invent decorative epithets.
- Use a supporting character's name only when their action changes information, plan, cost, rescue, or result.
- If two nearby characters share a role or gender, repeat the clearer label rather than risk ambiguity.

## Narrator-Stance Test

Before drafting, answer:

```text
what contrast the narrator notices first:
what the narrator calls dangerous, absurd, expensive, unfair, or ironic:
which visible reaction matters and why:
what information is withheld because nobody can know it yet:
what concise judgment could not appear in an encyclopedia summary:
```

If the passage becomes equally effective after deleting every judgment and contrast, the narrator is only a timeline reader.
