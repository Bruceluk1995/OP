# Chinese Women-Oriented Novel-Site Inspiration Protocol

Use this only when the user selects Chinese women-oriented novel sites, asks to search Chinese female-audience fiction for inspiration, or explicitly includes them in an all-source scan. This is an ideation layer for Japanese female-audience fantasy romance, not a shortcut for copying Chinese webnovels or proving Japanese demand.

## Source Role

- Browse current public ranking, category, tag, title, and short synopsis metadata from Chinese women-oriented fiction sites. Useful starting surfaces include 晋江文学城、番茄小说女频、起点女生网、潇湘书院、红袖添香、七猫女频, but use whichever current public pages are actually accessible.
- Prefer at least two independent sites or lists when describing a repeated emotional or relationship mechanism. Record source, page/list context, capture date and timezone, and any visible metric or missing field.
- Do not bypass login, paywalls, anti-bot controls, or copy full chapters. User-provided or legitimately accessible text may be analyzed only under the normal anti-copy rules.
- Treat one title or one list snapshot as an example. Treat repetition across sites, categories, or capture times as a Chinese-market signal only.

## Candidate Gate

1. Collect 6-10 current items and extract only the functional mechanism: heroine wound, heroine choice, relationship pressure, male-lead recognition, proof object, social consequence, emotional payoff, and ending promise.
2. Score each mechanism from 1-5 for heroine empathy, romance/zamaa engine, Japanese female-fantasy fit, localization distance, and originality space.
3. Present 3-5 viable numbered candidates. Include Chinese source/date, raw mechanism, proposed Japanese josei route, localization rebuild, Japan cross-check status, and risk note.
4. Stop for the user's numbered choice unless the user supplied the exact work/link/keyword or explicitly delegated selection.
5. After selection, cross-check the mechanism against at least one current Japanese ranking, tag, reader-interest, or comparable public signal before calling it Japanese-market fit. If no Japanese confirmation is found, label it `cross-market hypothesis` and propose a small test.

## Functional Rebuild

For the chosen seed, change the source-facing expression across these dimensions:

- heroine role, wound, agency, and desired future;
- male-lead role, recognition mode, vulnerability, and public choice;
- harem, 嫡庶, 冷宫, 赐婚, 宫斗, 侯府, 豪门, family hierarchy, and marriage functions into coherent Japanese female-fantasy institutions;
- proof object, reversal venue, legal/social consequence, and romance progression;
- title language, names, terminology, emotional aftertaste, and ending promise.

Pass only a compact function card into planning or drafting:


## Hard Boundaries

- Do not copy titles, names, dialogue, scene order, signature set pieces, proprietary systems, or a distinctive combination of plot turns.
- Do not transliterate Chinese palace, family, marriage, inheritance, revenge, cultivation, or modern wealthy-family machinery into Japanese costume.
- Do not present Chinese rankings, reads, or popularity as Japanese audience evidence.
- Do not let a Chinese source weaken heroine agency, turn control into 溺愛, or override the selected Japanese presentation, viewpoint, route, or one-shot/serial shape.
